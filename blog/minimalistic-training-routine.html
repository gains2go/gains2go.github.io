A minimalistic training routine

  03.04.2023

This is an example of a minimalistic training routine I use while travelling. I setup gymnastics rings on a tree on a nice beach and perform L-Sit muscle ups. This is one of the best minimalistic upper body excercises out there because you train a pulling, pushing and core movement in one excercise. I end each set with a couple of skin the cats to train my straight-arm-strength and finish the last set off with ring dips and pullups. 3 sets and 15 minutes later Iâ€˜m already done with a complete upper body workout.

The pulling phase of the L-Sit muscle up trains the lats, rhomboids, traps, biceps, forearms and abs. The transition phase focuses mostly on isometric triceps and scapular strength, but also on the abs and the whole shoulder girdle, especially the rear delts. The pushing phase emphasizes mostly the chest, the triceps and the front delts. Finally, lockout position at the top of the movement requires good isometric biceps strength and further stability in the whole shoulder girdle.

The skin-the-cats train mostly the lats, shoulders and the often neglected straight-arm strength in the biceps and triceps. Secondary muscles are the abs and the spinal erectos.

Overall this training routine encompasses all muscles of the upper body in both dynamic and isometric movement patterns. Astonishingly it can be completed in just 15 minutes.
